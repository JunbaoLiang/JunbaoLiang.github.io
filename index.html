About

I'm a Ph.D. candidate in Computational Chemistry at Virginia Tech, advised by Valerie Welborn.

My research generates molecular-scale signals from physics-based simulation and translates them into interpretable, predictive features. I care about the full path — from force-field dynamics to the model that a team can actually act on — and about doing it rigorously enough to trust the result.

Recently that's meant an XGBoost binding-pocket predictor reaching F1 ≈ 0.92, a published Protein Science study on enzyme glycosylation, and benchmark datasets for peptide hydration dynamics — work that bridges rigorous simulation and applied machine learning for drug discovery.

Selected work

Binding-pocket prediction from protein structure

2024

A grid-based pocket-detection pipeline with an XGBoost classifier trained on geometric and pharmacophore features from local protein neighborhoods — F1 ≈ 0.92 on held-out data. Scaled inference with cKDTree spatial indexing and exported probability-scored pockets as PDB for visualization.

  • XGBoost
  • Feature engineering
  • cKDTree
  • scikit-learn

Glycosylated enzyme to reduce product inhibition in DszB

2025 · published

Artificially glycosylated DszB with β-glucose and predicted loop-dynamics changes with AMOEBA MD. Connected glycation to reduced product trapping through RMSD/RMSF and Ramachandran analysis. Published in Protein Science.

  • AMOEBA MD
  • RMSD / RMSF
  • Tinker

Peptide hydration-water dynamics in glucose solution

2025 · ongoing

Built a benchmark dataset of solvated amino-acid pentamers across 0–2 M glucose to quantify composition-dependent hydration. Computed VACF-based diffusion, H-bond statistics, RDF, radius of gyration, and SASA to characterize glucose-induced dynamical shifts.

  • CHARMM
  • MDAnalysis
  • VACF · SASA

Electrolyte selection for sodium-ion batteries

2024 · in prep

Simulated sodium-ion electrolytes in diglyme (NaPF₆, NaOTF) with AMOEBA and CHARMM, quantifying ion transport and solvation structure via RDF and diffusion to rationalize experimental trends and guide electrolyte choice. Manuscript in preparation.

  • AMOEBA / CHARMM
  • GROMACS
  • RDF · diffusion
